Why Kali Strikes

Self-defense should feel practical, not performative.

A lot of women want real training. Not a fitness class rebranded as self-defense, and not a competitive martial arts program built for people who have trained for years. Something in between: structured, skill-based, beginner-friendly, and taught by someone who takes it seriously.

Kali and Arnis, the national martial arts of the Philippines, are built on practical principles. Distance management. Footwork. Awareness. Weapon and empty-hand training that transfers directly to real situations. It is approachable for beginners and offers genuine depth for those who continue.

Kali Strikes was built for women who want that kind of training, in a calm and focused environment, without the intimidation factor that keeps many people from walking through the door.

What You Will Learn

The Core Curriculum

Each session builds on the last. The goal is practical skill, not performance.

01

Stick Fundamentals

Grip, stance, and the basic striking angles used in Filipino stick fighting. This is the foundation. Every drill and every concept builds from here. No prior experience with weapons is assumed.

02

Footwork and Distance

How to move efficiently, maintain a safe distance, and reposition under pressure. Most confrontations are shaped before any contact is made. Footwork changes the equation.

03

Awareness and Boundary-Setting

Practical skills for reading environments, recognizing early warning signs, and communicating limits clearly. These habits apply in everyday life, not just crisis moments.

04

Partner Drills and Reaction Training

Controlled partner work that builds timing, coordination, and the ability to respond rather than freeze. All drills are supervised and structured around safety.

05

Pressure and Composure

Structured exercises that introduce mild stress so you can practice staying calm and making decisions under physical pressure. No sparring. No competition. No ego.

Seminar Format

What a Kali Strikes Seminar Looks Like

Each seminar follows a structured format designed to move participants from zero experience to a working understanding of the fundamentals in one session. Below is the typical half-day outline.

Full-day and custom formats are available. Contact us to discuss your group's specific needs.

  1. 01

    Welcome and Safety Briefing

    Approximately 10 minutes

    Equipment check, introductions, ground rules, and what to expect from the session. Everyone starts at the same place.

  2. 02

    Stance, Grip, and Footwork

    Approximately 20 minutes

    Foundation work. How to stand, hold a training stick, and move with intention. Fundamentals that every drill relies on.

  3. 03

    Kali Stick Drills

    Approximately 30 minutes

    Basic angles of attack and defense. Pattern work that builds muscle memory. Repetition over complexity.

  4. 04

    Self-Defense Concepts

    Approximately 20 minutes

    Distance management, awareness cues, and how to create space. Practical principles that apply when you do not have a stick in your hand.

  5. 05

    Partner Flow Work

    Approximately 20 minutes

    Applied drills with a partner in a controlled, supervised setting. Focus is on rhythm, timing, and comfort with contact.

  6. 06

    Q and A and Next Steps

    Approximately 10 minutes

    Open questions, resources, and information on how participants can continue their training.

About the Founder

Sensei Katrina Woodville

Chicagoland, Illinois

Sensei Katrina Woodville is a Chicagoland-based instructor specializing in Filipino martial arts and women's self-defense training. She began training in Kali and Arnis to develop practical tools for personal safety and has since focused her teaching on making those tools genuinely accessible to women with no martial arts background.

She founded Kali Strikes because most self-defense programs she encountered were either too sport-focused, too intense for beginners, or not designed with women's specific experiences in mind. Kali Strikes is her answer to that gap: structured, credible, and taught in an environment where beginners feel welcome from the first session.
